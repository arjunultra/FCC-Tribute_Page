Mahātmā Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

India's Father of the Nation

Here's a timeline of Mahātmā Gandhiji's life:

  1. Early Life and Education (1869-1888):
    • Gandhi was born to Karamchand Gandhi and Putlibai in Porbandar.
    • He grew up in a devout Hindu family and was influenced by Jain philosophy and his mother's teachings.
    • In 1888, he traveled to London to study law, where he encountered racism and began to develop his principles of justice and equality.
  2. Experiences in South Africa (1893-1914):
    • Gandhi moved to South Africa to work as a lawyer, where he witnessed racial discrimination against Indians.
    • He began his activism by leading campaigns against injustices, advocating for the rights of Indians, and launching his philosophy of nonviolent resistance, "Satyagraha."
  3. Return to India (1915-1919):
    • Gandhi returned to India and joined the Indian National Congress in its struggle for independence from British rule.
    • He organized various protests, strikes, and marches, advocating for civil rights and self-reliance.
    • The Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919 and the subsequent harsh British measures led to widespread protests and marked a turning point in India's fight for freedom.
  4. Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922):
    • Gandhi launched the Non-Cooperation Movement, urging Indians to boycott British goods, institutions, and symbols.
    • Millions of Indians participated, showcasing their commitment to nonviolence and civil disobedience.
    • The movement was suspended after incidents of violence, but it had a profound impact on India's political landscape.
  5. Salt March and Civil Disobedience (1930-1934):
    • Gandhi led the historic Salt March in 1930, a 240-mile protest against the British monopoly on salt production and sales.
    • The march gained international attention and fueled the civil disobedience movement.
    • Despite imprisonment and hardships, Gandhi's philosophy of nonviolence continued to inspire millions.
